PARTE: Plane-Assisted Robust Transformation Estimation for Point Cloud Registration

Point Cloud Registration Planes + Points Robotics

Abstract

We introduce PARTE (Plane-Assisted Robust Transformation Estimation), a registration algorithm that leverages planar patches alongside points. PARTE extracts planes, builds a compact contour-based plane descriptor, and fuses plane-plane and point-point matches in a single consistency graph, selecting inliers via a maximum-clique solver. An extended Arun SVD provides a closed-form rotation that incorporates plane normals, followed by a closed-form translation and a short nonlinear refinement. Across 5,153 pairs spanning 6 widely-used datasets, PARTE achieves the highest overall success rate (89%) at 403 ms mean runtime; the next best method yields 84% at 627 ms. PARTE also reaches 100% success on ETH and KITTI outdoor sequences while remaining fast.

Method

PARTE is a global, correspondence-based, structure-aware pipeline:

  • Preprocess: voxel downsample; robust plane segmentation to form planar patches.
  • Descriptors & matches: FPFH + MNN for points; Triangular Centroid Descriptor (TCD) on plane contours for plane matching.
  • Joint outlier rejection: a single consistency graph over point-point and plane-plane (and mixed) constraints; inliers via maximum clique (PMC).
  • Pose estimation: extended Arun SVD with plane normals for rotation, closed-form translation, then brief LM/BCD refinement.

Results

Evaluated on indoor/outdoor RGB-D, LiDAR, and TLS datasets (104-106 points/scan), PARTE ranks first on 11/18 sequences and second on the rest, with consistent speedups from plane compression. Outdoors, a single dominant plane (e.g., ground) often suffices for near-perfect alignment; indoors, planes reduce ambiguity and runtime. Representative numbers: 89% success @ 403 ms across all pairs; ETH 100% RR; KITTI 100% RR.
